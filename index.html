The goal for my future is to explore the bridge between computers and brains, not only has an analogy, but also as an extension of the mind in two different mediums.

CRITICAL THINKING

It is a great skill to be able to process information that is transmitted to us from other mental entities. Anything spoken or written is nothing more than some else's opinion, and we process information accordingly to our own set of beliefs. It is important that we have present which sub set of beliefs we are using to process any given information. We should be conscious that for as much confidence we have in something, it can be wrong. In an information sharing setting, each person should contribute with their opinion to persuade the other to change his set of beliefs. Since we can be wrong, it is import to be flexible and critic about one own set of beliefs.

EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE

Our emotions are powerful resources that influence our behavior. This power can take control of our actions or be used as a guide for our decisions. It is important to be aware of our emotions, so we can understand what is causing that emotion. This allows the mind to decide where to spend the energy and invest the mental resources in actions that serve our goals. Mindfulness techniques are a great tool to train our mind on how to recognize our emotions. My mindfulness practices have taught me how to maintain clear focus in stressful situations, specially in situations where my emoticons of panic and fear kept informing me about the dangers and worries.
